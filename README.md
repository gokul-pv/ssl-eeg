# MAI MSc Thesis

## Description
This is a template repo describing the common expectations for master thesis developped under the supervision of Magda Gregorova.

## Usage
You can use this template to create a new repo within the [thws-mai](https://github.com/thws-mai) organization and use it as a basis for your master thesis.
Call the repo `MScThesisxx_shortThesisName`, where `xx` stands for the year of the thesis finalization and you replace `shortThesisName` by an understandable short name for your thesis, e.g. `MScThesis25_SVGGeneration`. In principal, the repo shall be public.

For more info on using repo templates see [Creating a repository from a template](https://docs.github.com/en/repositories/creating-and-managing-repositories/creating-a-repository-from-a-template).

All artifacts related to the thesis shall be stored within this repo. This includes the manuscript, the code as well as all working material.

The repo proposes a basic folder structure: 
* `code` - all related software and documentation, e.g. readme with dependencies/requirements and user instructions
* `manuscript` - manuscript including all originals such as tex files and images as well as the final pdf version
* `workings` - all working material for the thesis
  * `minutes` - for keeping meeting minutes
  * `proposal` - for initial thesis proposal

You can extend the folder structure as you see fit.

Large files such as data, videos etc. shall not be stored within the repo. Ask Magda to create a folder for you on the `fiw.cloud` for these.

## Master thesis organization

### Rule #1: it is your master thesis!
You shall assume the active role in all parts of the thesis organization and development. This includes but is not limited to all parts of thesis planning, definition of concrete thesis outcomes, strategy for development, meeting organization, thesis documentation and communication with external parties. 

My role as a supervisor is to assist you in this process. I can advise you on all the above but the final decisions as well as their execution is your responsibility. Of course within the limits of good academic conduct.

### Working time
The thesis is worth 25 ECTS which corresponds to 750 study hours spread over the span of 6 months. This is equivalent to a bit over 30 hours per week - so almost to a full time job - the expectations on the thesis outcomes are correspondingly high and you shall. You shall think well, if you wish to have an additional job on the side and how you organize your time in the week.

### Communication
There are three basic types of communication between the you and myself with differing structure and flexibility:

* meetings: typically organized by you. You shall propose a meeting date using the email scheduling tool to find a free slot, block it in our calendars, specify the meeting objectives/goals and prepare an agenda. 
  * The planning and scheduling of the meetings is your responsibility. My recommendation is regular weekly updates subject to my availability limited by the need to attend to some other important matters or traveling.  
  * You shall prepare the supporting material for the meeting and talk me through it during the meeting. In case of questions, think them through before the meeting so we can answer them quickly and efficiently.
  * Meetings shall not extend 1 hour. Please prepare carefully to avoid delays caused by you improvising or searching for the background material.
  * Meetings shall be recorded within meeting minutes for future reference - see above the folder structure. 
  * I have a strong preference for physical meetings, generally in CAIRO. 
* [email](mailto:magda.gregorova@thws.de): for more complex questions, communication. I tend to respond within the same day.
* [mattermost](https://mattermost.fiw.fhws.de/): quick messaging, I tend to respond within a couple of minutes.
If you are not yet on mattermost, here an invite [link](https://mattermost.fiw.fhws.de/signup_user_complete/?id=tca4uuxfeprdxeaeoc57hakqqr&md=link&sbr=su).
  
### Thesis planning and management
Remember that the thesis has to be finalized within 6 months. Having a clear timeline with internal deadlines and milestones is therefore highly recommendable. Try to think about all things that need to be done straight from the beginning, list them as tasks with estimated time / work effort the best you can and plan them well ahead of time.

The work plan and timelines will need to be adapted as the thesis develops so be prepared to be flexible. We shall briefly discuss the time-plan and your current status at every meeting.

### Working place and internal collaboration
You can decide freely when and where you wish to work on thesis.

These are some possible options:

* CAIRO ground floor student room & Expo room - available freely on first-come-first-serve basis during the usual extended CAIRO working hours (8am-10pm, Mon-Sat)
* CAIRO offices - available for regular work during usual working hours (8:30am-6pm Mon-Fri). Advantages: common working space encouraging collaboration, some hardware support (larger screens), access to me or my PhDs for quick consultations. Disadvantages: limited capacity, only accessible with my PhDs or myself in the building.
